Your Help in Need!

Since ILEE_CSK aims to boost accuracy and efficiency of image analysis of cytoskeleton, which should echoes the demand of the communittee, we are very happy to learn critical information of related image process algorithms, bugs, and your demand. You are welcomed to send an email to announce any of the following issues: lipai@msu.edu

This page will be continuously renewed parallel to our library update.


A general sparse linear solver on GPU

ILEE need a sparse linear solver, which means GPU acceleration make it 10-20 times faster. For GPU acceleration mode, we are using MATLAB and CUDA, but it requires users to have MATLAB license and a CUDA-compatible GPU, which is not general enough. While we did some search on the Internet, we could not find any GPU based linear solver that is general and open-sourced. We wish you can recommend us anything that works as a better substitute for our current solution. We prefer to use conditional gradient (CG) algorithm for linear solve because we found it has the best performance in our tests.


Upgrading skeletonization algorithm

Many cytoskeleton indices depends on its skeleton image (don't confuse with "cytoskeleton", it means image rendered by thinning binary image to one-pixel but remain topological structure.). We are using the Skeletonize function in the scikit-image, which is very slow in 3D mode. It is perhaps because this function uses a single process on CPU (mine is 10-20% CPU usage), and therefore the computational power is not used efficiently. We would like you to recommend us a new function/library for pixel/voxel image skeletonization that uses a faster algorithm or multi-processing mode.


Bug report

If you follows our tutorial/API and find bugs, please report to us. Better send us your image sample and tell us how you process the sample in detail so that we can repeat.


Tell us your demand

If our algorithm does not fulfil your demand, you are welcomed to tell us what you wish to learn from your cytoskeleton image of your research or what functions you expect to have for our library. We aim to incorporate all possible functions for the interest of the community and therefore need your input.
